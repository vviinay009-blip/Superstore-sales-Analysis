### **Retail Sales \& Profitability Analysis (SQL + Excel)**### #### Project SummaryThis project examines a retail sales dataset comprising 9,994 transaction records to extract insights on sales, customer behaviour, profitability, and discount strategies. The process included data cleaning, importing data, SQL analysis, and creating an interactive Excel dashboard.##### Tools Utilised  \- MySQL  \- Power Query  \- Microsoft Excel  ##### Dataset Details  \- Dataset: Sample Superstore  \- Total Records: 9,994  \- Order Level: Transaction line-item  \- Unique Orders: 5,009  \- Unique Customers: 793  ###### Data Preparation  \- Imported the raw dataset into Excel  \- Adjusted data to ensure compatibility with SQL  \- Verified all 9,994 records  \- Checked for missing values and errors prior to SQL import  ###### ###### Business Questions Addressed  \- What are the total sales and profits?  \- Which category generates the highest revenue and profit?  \- Which customer segments contribute the most to sales?  \- How does the discount rate impact profitability?  \- How do performance metrics vary across regions and categories?  ###### Dashboard Capabilities  \- KPI cards  \- Sales by category  \- Profit by category  \- Sales segment distribution  \- Discount versus profit analysis  \- Interactive slicers for region, segment, and category linked to all features for in-depth analysis##### Detailed Regional analysis**Central Region**  \- Furniture: Sales are lower and appear to be causing losses.  \- Office Supplies: Sales are higher compared to furniture and seem to yield moderate profits, possibly due to greater discount rates.  \- Technology: Despite offering the lowest discount rates, it leads in both sales and profits among the three categories.**East Region**  \- Furniture: Sales are higher than in the central region, but profits remain low.  \- Office Supplies: Performing well with strong profits in this region.  \- Technology: Again, the highest in sales and profits despite the lowest discount rates.**South Region**  \- Furniture: Achieves a reasonable sales volume and moderate profits.  \- Office Supplies: Matches technology in profit generation and overall stands out as the top performer in this area.  \- Technology: Maintains the highest sales and solid profits.**West Region**  \- Furniture: Demand is strongest here, generating moderate profits.  \- Office Supplies: Although demand is lower than furniture and technology, it contributes the largest share of profits among the three.  \- Technology: Exhibits high sales accompanied by good profits.The analysis clearly highlights the strengths and opportunities across regions and product categories, making a compelling case for strategic focus and investment tailored to each market’s unique performance.&#x20;
